NEXA tracks an opportunity from first lead all the way through pricing, approval, SoW/PO, and project-code generation. Most things happen automatically as a deal moves forward — this guide explains what to expect and where you need to take action.

01 The opportunity lifecycle

Every opportunity moves through a fixed sequence of stages. The stage advances on its own as you complete each step (create pricing, get approval, upload documents, etc.).

WLBU Pending→ Price Linking→ Price Linked→ Approval Pending→ SOW Pending→ SOW Submitted→ SOW Under Review→ Archiving

Some tabs stay greyed-out until the deal reaches the right point — this is intentional, so steps happen in order. For example, the SOW / PO tab opens up once a pricing approval request has been submitted, and Project Code opens after SoW verification.

02 Status vs. Stage — two different things

Stage is the workflow position (above) and moves automatically. Status is the business outcome and is set manually from the dropdown on the opportunity:

  • Open · Won · Lost · Abandoned
Note Approving the SoW verification does not automatically mark a deal “Won”. Status is always a deliberate choice — set it yourself. (Lost and Abandoned opportunities are excluded from the pipeline-revenue figures on the dashboard.)

03 Line of Business is computed for you

You don’t pick a Line of Business when creating an opportunity. NEXA works it out automatically: it looks at the staffing mix in the final pricing version and takes the dominant (majority) LoB domain — the one carrying the largest share of the revenue mix.

  • The opportunity’s Primary LoB fills in once you mark a pricing version as final.
  • In the pricing Details, the highlighted chip in the “LoB Revenue Mix” is the one used as the Primary LoB.
  • Full names are shown on the Details tab (e.g. Managed Services, Data Science) rather than the short codes.

04 Pricing & versions

  • An opportunity can have several pricing versions; you mark one as final.
  • Once a version is final and the deal has entered approval, the pricing becomes read-only (locked) so numbers can’t shift mid-approval.
  • If pricing must change after approval, mark a different version final — this invalidates the previous approval and a fresh request is needed.
Heads up Opportunities with a longer time window (multi-year, many weeks of staffing) have a lot more to calculate and save — expect these to take noticeably longer to open and save than short engagements. Give it a moment rather than clicking repeatedly.

05 Approvals

  • There are two kinds: Pricing Approval and SoW / PO / PCA Verification.
  • The chosen approver receives an email with Approve / Reject buttons — they can act straight from the email, or from the Approvals page.
  • People added in CC get a notification only (no action buttons).
  • The requester can withdraw a pending request.
  • The Approvals inbox has a Pending | Historical toggle — Pending shows what’s awaiting a decision, Historical shows everything already approved, rejected, or withdrawn.
Note Verifying the SoW/PO/PCA advances the deal to project-code generation; it does not set the status to Won (see section 2).

06 SoW / PO & pre-contract agreement

To progress a deal you provide either signed documents or a pre-contract agreement — not both:

  • You may upload a SoW and a PO together.
  • Or tick “Proceed with pre-contract agreement”.
Important — mutually exclusive Pre-contract agreement and SoW/PO documents cannot both be used. If you’ve uploaded a document, the pre-contract checkbox is disabled; if pre-contract is ticked, the upload areas are disabled. To switch routes, remove the uploaded documents first.

Documents accepted: PDF, Word, Excel, PNG, JPEG — up to roughly 49 MB each.

07 Clients & client requests

  • Anyone can request a new client while creating an opportunity.
  • These requests are reviewed and approved on the Client master page (the admin requests panel there). Approving one creates the client record with its ID.
  • The Client master is also where you browse existing clients and add new ones directly.

08 Rate cards

  • Rate cards are uploaded from an Excel file in the specified format — keep the column layout and the sheet naming intact (the upload looks for the “Rate Card” sheet).
  • When updating, add new entries as new rows below the existing ones — don’t reorder or remove the existing columns/headers.
  • After uploading, you confirm the parsed entries before they’re saved.
Heads up If the format or sheet name doesn’t match, the upload won’t recognise the data. When in doubt, start from the last working file and append rows.

09 Sync & download (OneDrive / SharePoint)

From the dashboard you can Sync to OneDrive (updates the shared SharePoint Excel) or Download a local copy.

Important — close the file first Before syncing, the shared OneDrive/SharePoint file must be closed everywhere — in your browser, the desktop Excel app, and on any colleague’s machine. If it’s open anywhere it stays locked and the sync will fail. Close it, then sync.
  • It’s a full refresh, not a merge. Each sync rewrites the whole file to match NEXA exactly. Removed opportunities disappear; the file always reflects current data.
  • Don’t hand-edit the shared file. Any manual changes are overwritten on the next sync.
  • 5,000-row limit. The export contains only the most recent 5,000 opportunities (newest first). Older ones beyond that aren’t included in the file.
  • Download gives you a timestamped copy on your PC (e.g. NEXA_Export_2026-06-02_15-30-45.xlsx) — handy for a point-in-time snapshot.
