01 What NEXA is

NEXA is ProcDNA's internal tool to track a business-development opportunity from first lead through pricing → approval → SoW/PO → project-code generation. It replaces spreadsheets for building a staffing-based price, getting it approved, attaching contract documents, and reporting pipeline.

The spine of the product is: create an opportunity → build one or more pricing versions (staffing + other costs) → mark one final → get it approved → upload signed SoW/PO → verify → archive. Almost everything is driven off that flow.

03 The lifecycle (stages)

Every opportunity moves through a fixed stage sequence. The stage advances automatically as each step is completed — you don't set it by hand.

WLBU Pending→ Price Linking→ Price Linked→ Approval Pending→ SOW Pending→ SOW Submitted→ SOW Under Review→ Archiving
StageMeansNext step
WLBU PendingNew lead, no pricing yetCreate a pricing version
Price LinkingA version exists, not finalisedMark a version as final
Price LinkedA final version is setSend for pricing approval
Approval PendingPricing sent, awaiting approverApprover approves / rejects
SOW PendingPricing approvedUpload SoW/PO or mark pre-contract
SOW SubmittedA document / pre-contract is inSend for SoW verification

04 Status vs. Stage — two separate things

Stage = workflow position (above), moves automatically. Status = the business outcome, set manually: Open · Won · Lost · Abandoned.

Remember Approving SoW verification does not auto-mark the deal "Won" — someone sets the status deliberately. Lost and Abandoned opportunities are excluded from the dashboard's pipeline-revenue figures.

  • Opportunities table with search and column filters (status, win %, time window, next step).
  • Win % column shows the deal's probability — it jumps to 100% once SoW verification is accepted.

06 The pricing engine (the logic to understand)

  • Staffing → money. Each resource has a cost rate, a bill rate, and weekly hours. The tool sums these into Proposed Billings, Cost, Gross Margin, Hours, Offshore %, etc.
  • Utilization ↔ hours are linked (both ways). 100% = 40h in a full 5-day week (8h/day). 50.5% → 20.2h. Enter a utilization and the weekly hours auto-fill; type hours manually and the utilization is back-calculated as Σ hours / (working-days × 8) × 100.
  • Edge weeks are prorated. A partial first/last week is filled by its real working days — e.g. a project starting Thursday gets a 2-day (16h) first week, not 40h.
  • Monthly split is by working days. Financial & Schedule-of-Payments figures divide across months by each month's working-day count — not by calendar weeks — so partial months are accurate and totals always reconcile.
  • Primary LoB is auto-computed as the dominant Line of Business (by revenue) in the final pricing's staffing mix.

07 Approvals flow

  • Two kinds: Pricing Approval and SoW / PO / PCA Verification.
  • The chosen approver gets an email with Approve / Reject buttons (CC'd people are notified only).
  • Approve advances the stage; reject returns it for revision; the requester can withdraw a pending request.
  • Verifying SoW/PO/PCA moves the deal to project-code generation — it does not set the status to Won.

08 Key rules & behaviours

Important Changing an opportunity's dates resets its pricing. All staffing hours are cleared and the opportunity goes back to "Price Linked" — the pricing must be rebuilt for the new window and sent for approval again. (Blocked while an approval is actively in progress.)
Important — mutually exclusive A deal progresses on either uploaded SoW/PO documents or a pre-contract agreement — never both. Picking one disables the other.
  • Pricing locks once a version is final and an approval is in progress, so numbers can't shift mid-review. To change pricing after approval, mark a different version final — this invalidates the prior approval and needs a fresh request.
  • Win % → 100% automatically once SoW verification is accepted.
  • Client IDs are assigned by finance/admins (e.g. 1004VL3002), not auto-generated. Until assigned, the client shows "Pending ID".
  • Documents accepted: PDF / Word / Excel / PNG / JPEG, up to ~49 MB each.

09 Important notes & gotchas for the PM

OneDrive sync Before syncing, the shared OneDrive/SharePoint Excel must be closed everywhere (browser, desktop, colleagues) or it's locked and the sync fails. The sync is a full overwrite — manual edits to that file are lost — and it includes only the most recent 5,000 opportunities.
Performance Opportunities with a long time window (multi-year, many weeks of staffing) take noticeably longer to open and save — there's a lot to compute. The save/finalise popups say so; give it a moment rather than re-clicking.
